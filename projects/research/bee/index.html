    Population Structure and Landscape Genetics of Honey Bees

    2011 - 2013 Aarhus University, Slagelse, Denmark

    This project investigated the population structure and spatial distribution of honey bees (Apis mellifera spp.) mostly in Europe and to a lesser extend in Africa. DNA microsatellites were used in conjunction with Bayesian assignment and Principal Component Analysis. The aim of the project was discovery of novel genotypes, understanding admixture in natural populations and well as estimation of anthropogenic effects,Â conservation of small and endemic populations, enforcement of beekeeping restrictions and identification of illegal importation.
